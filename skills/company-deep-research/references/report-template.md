# Company Deep Research Report Template

## Header

- Company:
- Ticker / exchange:
- Report date:
- Latest completed trading-day close:
- Research purpose:
- Position context, if provided:
- Disclaimer: research support only, not financial advice.

## 1. Executive View

- One-sentence investment logic.
- Rating posture: priority research, watch, cautious, avoid.
- Main risks first.
- What would change the view.

## 2. Company Profile

- Basic information.
- Main businesses and revenue mix over the last 3 years.
- Geography, customers, and product lines.
- Corporate actions or governance events that matter.

## 3. Industry Analysis

- Industry size and growth.
- Lifecycle stage.
- Competitive structure and concentration.
- Pricing dynamics and profit pools.
- Upstream/downstream bargaining power.

## 4. Business Model and Moat

- Customer, product, pricing, repeat purchase, scale effect.
- Moat review: brand, cost, network effect, patents, channel control, switching cost.
- Moat score: strong, medium, weak, with disconfirming evidence.

## 5. Financial Analysis

Use at least 5 years when available.

- Revenue and profit CAGR.
- Gross margin, operating margin, net margin.
- ROE and DuPont drivers.
- Operating cash flow versus net profit.
- Free cash flow: operating cash flow minus capex.
- Receivables, inventory, working capital.
- Leverage, interest burden, short-term debt risk.

## 6. Management and Governance

- Management background and stability.
- Capital allocation: dividends, buybacks, M&A, capex, expansion.
- Ownership, pledges, dilution, related-party risks.

## 7. Valuation

### Current Valuation

- Latest completed trading-day close, date, and source.
- PE TTM, PB, PEG, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield where applicable.

### Intrinsic Value

- Base free cash flow definition and source.
- Share count source.
- Scenarios: conservative, base, optimistic.
- Discount rate, explicit growth, terminal growth.
- Per-share value range.
- Sensitivity table or stress test.

### Safety Margin and Action Bands

Only provide executable bands if the valuation anchor is supportable.

- Buy/add range.
- Hold/watch range.
- Reduce range.
- Avoid/sell discipline.
- Position sizing and fundamental stop conditions.

## 8. Risks and Disconfirming Signals

- Industry cycle risk.
- Policy and regulation.
- Competition and price war.
- Financial quality.
- Governance.
- Model and data limitations.

## 9. Scorecard

Suggested 100-point frame:

- Business model and moat: 30.
- Financial quality: 30.
- Financial safety: 15.
- Management: 10.
- Valuation safety margin: 15.

## 10. Bibliography

List source, date, URL or local path, and the exact data used.
