# K-Line Horizon Framework

## Evidence Checklist

### Price Structure

- Bullish: higher highs and higher lows, successful retests, range breakout.
- Neutral: overlapping candles, failed directional follow-through, range-bound swings.
- Bearish: lower highs and lower lows, failed bounces, breakdown and weak retest.

### Moving Averages

- Short term: 5/10/20-day.
- Medium term: 20/60-day.
- Long term: 120/250-day or weekly/monthly equivalents.
- Strong alignment: price above rising averages in correct order.
- Weak alignment: averages flat, tangled, or price repeatedly crossing.
- Bearish alignment: price below falling averages.

### Volume

- Breakout is stronger with volume expansion.
- Breakdown is more serious with volume expansion.
- Rising price on shrinking volume needs caution.
- Capitulation volume can mark panic, but needs follow-through.

### Momentum and Volatility

- RSI/MACD divergence can warn but does not confirm reversal alone.
- ATR expansion after breakdown implies higher risk.
- Volatility contraction near resistance/support can precede expansion; wait for direction.

## Scorecard

Score each horizon from -2 to +2:

```text
+2: bullish trend confirmed by structure, moving averages, and volume.
+1: bullish bias but missing confirmation.
 0: range or conflicting evidence.
-1: weakening bias but not confirmed breakdown.
-2: bearish trend confirmed by structure, moving averages, and volume.
```

Confidence:

- High: 4+ evidence types align and invalidation is clear.
- Medium: 2-3 evidence types align.
- Low: signals conflict, data is incomplete, or the move is news-driven.
