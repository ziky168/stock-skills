# Sector Cycle Indicator Map

## Evidence Clusters

### Demand

- Orders, shipments, sales volume, passenger/freight traffic, product consumption.
- Downstream capex plans, tender data, customer inventory restocking.
- Macro drivers: property, infrastructure, exports, consumer demand, rates, credit.

### Supply and Capacity

- Capacity additions or shutdowns.
- Capacity utilization and operating rates.
- Capex cycle, new entrants, import/export arbitrage.
- Policy constraints, permits, environmental limits.

### Prices and Spreads

- Main product prices.
- Upstream raw material prices.
- Product-minus-input spreads.
- Futures curve, spot premium/discount, freight rates where relevant.

### Inventory

- Producer inventory.
- Channel inventory.
- Customer inventory.
- Days of inventory and destocking/restocking evidence.

### Profitability

- Gross margin trend.
- Earnings revisions.
- Profit warnings or positive preannouncements.
- Cash flow conversion and receivable stress.

### Market Behavior

- Sector relative strength versus benchmark.
- Volume and turnover.
- Valuation percentile.
- Crowding, fund flows, margin financing, analyst upgrades.

### Policy and Events

- Subsidies, regulation, trade restrictions, safety checks.
- Tender rules, pricing mechanism changes, capacity control.
- Earnings season, industry conferences, high-frequency releases.

## Stage Heuristics

### Bottoming

- Demand no longer deteriorates.
- Inventory drawdown slows.
- Valuation and positioning are depressed.
- Bad news produces smaller price declines.

### Early Upturn

- Product prices or spreads stop falling.
- Orders improve before reported earnings.
- Leading companies outperform.
- Market narrative shifts from survival to recovery.

### Expansion

- Demand, prices, volume, and margins improve together.
- Earnings revisions turn positive.
- Pullbacks hold key support.
- Supply has not yet overexpanded.

### Topping

- Prices are high but volume or spreads flatten.
- Inventory accumulates.
- Capacity announcements accelerate.
- Good news fails to lift prices.
- Crowding and valuation are stretched.

### Contraction

- Prices/spreads, orders, and utilization weaken.
- Earnings cuts follow.
- Inventory rises or destocking intensifies.
- Rallies fail quickly.
