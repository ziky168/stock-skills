# Sector Cycle Analysis

## Overview

Use this skill to decide where a sector sits in its cycle and what must be watched next. The core rule is triangulation: never classify a cycle stage from price action alone. Combine industry fundamentals, upstream/downstream signals, market behavior, and disconfirming evidence.

This is research support only, not financial advice.

## Required Inputs

If missing, infer and label assumptions:

- Sector or industry definition and key listed proxies.
- Market and benchmark.
- Time horizon: weekly monitoring, quarterly industry call, or long-cycle thesis.
- Relevant upstream/downstream commodities, products, or customer demand drivers.
- Whether the user has related holdings.

## Stage Taxonomy

Use these default stages unless the user provides a house framework:

- **Bottoming:** bad news is priced in, valuation/positioning is low, and marginal data stops worsening.
- **Early Upturn:** expectations improve, capital starts entering, but earnings confirmation is incomplete.
- **Expansion:** earnings, price, demand, and trend confirmation align.
- **Topping:** crowding is high, good news stops working, and marginal data weakens.
- **Contraction:** demand, prices, earnings, or liquidity weaken; rebounds are fragile.

## Required Workflow

1. **Define the chain.** Map upstream costs, midstream producers, downstream customers, substitutes, and policy drivers.
2. **Collect evidence clusters.** Use at least four clusters when possible: demand, supply/capacity, prices/spreads, inventory, profitability, valuation, funds/market behavior, and policy.
3. **Check leading versus lagging signals.** Prices, orders, inventories, capacity utilization, and earnings revisions usually lead reported profits.
4. **Classify the stage.** Pick one primary stage and one alternate stage. Explain why the alternate could be true.
5. **State disconfirming signals.** List 2-4 data points that would invalidate the stage call.
6. **Link to action discipline.** Translate the cycle view into watch/add/reduce/avoid posture, position sizing caution, and review cadence.
7. **Create the next-monitor list.** Name the next 3-6 indicators/events and when to review them.

## Output Contract

Minimum output:

- Sector and date.
- Cycle stage: primary and alternate.
- Evidence table: signal, current observation, stage implication, source.
- Upstream/downstream price map.
- Key leaders or proxies.
- Disconfirming signals.
- Action posture and next review checklist.

For detailed indicators, read `references/indicator-map.md`.

## Common Mistakes

- Do not classify a sector as expanding only because the index rose.
- Do not ignore upstream cost pressure when downstream demand looks strong.
- Do not use one company as the whole industry unless it is a clear proxy.
- Do not omit the alternate stage; ambiguous cycles are normal.
- Do not hide data gaps. Name them and suggest the fastest validation path.
